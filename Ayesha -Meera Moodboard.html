Note — The Mix

Why Not Both?

Version A and Version B are not competing — they are two truths about the same clothes. The most powerful shoot plan is one that moves fluidly between both: open at Nahargarh with the city behind you, walk through Johari Bazaar mid-morning, then finish with a rooftop session at golden hour where the same Leheriya kurta that looked festival-ready at Gaitore now looks impossibly chic over an evening chai. The collection earns its commercial reach precisely because it lives in both worlds. Plan for 60% heritage shoot (Version A) and 40% everyday life (Version B) — the mix tells the complete brand story.

05 — Special Feature

The Teej Module

Hariyali Teej is the soul context of this entire shoot. Leheriya is worn by women across Rajasthan specifically during Sawan — making this collection not just fashion, but cultural participation. This module covers four distinct Teej shooting opportunities, each a different visual grammar.

5A — The Jhaki: Shooting Inside the Royal Procession

The Teej procession begins at City Palace and moves through Tripolia Gate → Chhoti Chaupar → Gangauri Bazaar. The procession includes the golden palanquin of Goddess Teej Mata, royal guards, folk dancers, decorated camels, and military bands. Models dressed in Leheriya outfits are photographed within the crowd lining the route — not staging the procession, but existing inside it. They look like festival participants, not models. The palanquin, marigold-decked arches, and old city's pink walls become the background.

Practical note: Keep the team small — one photographer, one assistant, three models. No tripod, no reflector. Scout the route on the day before and identify 3 anchor spots along Gangauri Bazaar (arched doorways make the best frame). Models arrive already dressed; no onset changes. Shoot window is 5–8 PM on the procession evening.

Leheriya looks only Team of 5 max Scout route day before Documentary style · no poses

5B — The Jhula: Decorated Swing at Sisodia Rani Ka Bagh

Women swinging on decorated jhulaas hung from trees draped with marigold strings and mango leaves is a defining Teej ritual visual. Set up a decorated swing in the garden at Sisodia Rani Ka Bagh — hung from one of the large tree boughs along the central axis. One model on the swing (in Leheriya green-yellow), two models beside her laughing — this is both a Teej ritual reference and a natural group composition. The garden's terraced backdrop and fountain channel make it even more spectacular.

Sisodia Rani Ka Bagh — garden area Prop: Decorated jhula, marigold garlands Natural group of 3 composition

5C — The Teej Puja Setup: Group Ritual Shot

A group shot rooted in the actual Hariyali Teej puja ritual. Three models seated together around a traditional puja arrangement — a decorated brass kalash with mango leaves and coconut, a brass thali with diya, marigold and rose flowers, kumkum, and sindoor. One model performing aarti, one with hands joined in prayer, one with mehendi hands holding flower petals. This is the most emotionally resonant shot concept — it's devotion, sisterhood, and festive beauty all in one frame.

Best set outdoors against the wall of Sisodia Rani Ka Bagh (painted with Krishna murals) or in a haveli courtyard. Late afternoon light, approximately 4 PM.

Brass Kalash + Mango leaves Brass Puja Thali Diyas (5–7 lit) Kumkum & Sindoor Marigold & Rose petals Red cloth ground cover Incense sticks Shiv-Parvati idol or photo

5D — Teej Bazaar & Mehendi Detail Shots

Johari Bazaar lifestyle shots — models at lac bangle stalls, mehendi being applied, hands holding Ghewar sweet. These feed directly into social media reels and Instagram Stories content around the Teej launch. Also include macro/detail shots: gota patti border close-up with mehendi hands in frame, lac bangles stacked against the Leheriya fabric, a brass diya near the kurta hem. These are the product-story shots that convert on e-commerce.

06 — Accessories

The Complete Shringar

Kundan Jhumka + Borla Tikka
Statement Kundan jhumkas with the signature Rajasthani bell-shaped borla maang tikka. Mandatory for Anarkali and Lehenga looks.
Lac Bangles — Teej Green
Traditional lac (shellac) bangles in Teej green and saffron. Full arm stacks for Leheriya looks. Green lac bangles are the definitive Teej accessory.
Embroidered Juttis
Gold mirror-work or gota patti embroidered juttis. The ultimate Rajasthani footwear — practical for outdoor locations, traditionally paired with every ethnic silhouette.
Gota Patti Potli Bag
Embroidered potli in matching or complementing fabric. Festive carry-piece that works as a detail prop for editorial shots. On-brand with the collection.
Gajra (Jasmine Flowers)
Fresh jasmine string woven into braided hair or hair bun. Essential for Teej looks — fragrant, traditional, and deeply Rajasthani. Adds softness to strong silhouettes.
Mehendi — Teej Ritual Hands
Applied the morning of the Teej special shoot. Hands shown in close-up shots with the gota border of the garment. Mehendi + gota = the definitive Teej detail shot.

12 — Post-Production Direction

The Grade & Edit Philosophy

Color Grade — Overall Direction

Warm, earthy, saturated. The Rajasthani palette should sing — deepen the Bandhani reds and Leheriya greens/saffrons. Lift the shadows just enough to preserve sandstone warmth. Keep skin tones rich and true — no over-brightening, no bleaching. Think: Rajasthani miniature painting translated to photography.

Retouching Philosophy

This is a plus-size brand that celebrates bodies as they are. Retouching philosophy: skin texture kept natural, no body reshaping whatsoever, only colour and light refinement. Meera's audience responds most to authentic, unmanipulated bodies. This is both ethical and commercial.

Per-Context Grade

  • Heritage locations — warm golden, slight film grain, lifted shadows
  • Teej procession — high saturation, vibrant festival colours, slight contrast boost
  • Desert (Kishanbagh) — sandy amber tones, deep shadows, cinematic
  • Garden (Sisodia Rani) — lush greens, soft morning light, romantic glow

Output Formats

  • E-commerce: white/neutral bg isolation, clean product view
  • Campaign: full landscape editorial, 16:9 and 4:5 crops
  • Instagram Grid: square 1:1 crop for feed cohesion
  • Reels: 9:16 vertical, shot list includes vertical-frame versions
  • Lookbook PDF: double-spread landscape masters
